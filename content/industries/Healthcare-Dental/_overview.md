# Healthcare & Dental: Umbrella Positioning

## Shared Themes Across Medical & Dental

Healthcare providers—whether traditional medical practices (imaging centers, ASCs) or dental groups (DSOs)—share fundamental characteristics that make them ideal targets for data analytics and operational intelligence:

### Common Pain Points

| Pain Point | Medical Manifestation | Dental Manifestation |
|------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **Hospital/DSO Competition** | Independent providers squeezed by hospital systems | Independent practices squeezed by DSO consolidation or competing with larger DSOs |
| **Margin Visibility** | No per-case/per-procedure profitability view | No per-practice/per-provider profitability view |
| **Payer Pressure** | Unfavorable contracts, no data for negotiation | Insurance dependency, reimbursement pressure |
| **Operational Inefficiency** | Denials, no-shows, scheduling gaps invisible | Chair utilization, case acceptance, recall effectiveness unmeasured |
| **Data Silos** | EMR, billing, scheduling don't connect | PMS, imaging, billing don't connect |
| **PE Reporting Needs** | Multi-site platforms need standardized visibility | Multi-location DSOs need unified analytics |

### Why Data/Analytics is the Right Solution

Both segments are:
- **Operationally complex**: Multiple revenue streams, payer types, procedure types
- **Margin-sensitive**: Small percentage improvements = significant dollar impact
- **Consolidating via PE**: Professional management demanding better visibility
- **Competing against larger players**: Need data to compete, can't afford enterprise solutions
- **Underserved by generic tools**: EMR/PMS reports are basic; enterprise platforms are overkill

### The Databender Opportunity

> **"Your data and analytics team—without the department."**

We provide on-demand expertise that independent and growing healthcare/dental organizations can't afford to build internally:
- Per-location/per-provider profitability analytics
- Competitive positioning against hospitals/large DSOs

## PE-Backed Targeting Priority

Both Medical and Dental segments are heavily PE-consolidated. PE-backed targets are priority because:

| PE Characteristic | Why It Helps |
|-------------------|--------------|
| Professional management | Decision-makers who speak "ROI" and "EBITDA" |
| Mandate to improve margins | Urgent need for visibility, not just interest |
| Multi-location portfolios | Larger deal sizes, standardization needs |
| Board reporting requirements | Need dashboards and KPIs, recurring need |
| Exit timeline (3-5 years) | Willing to invest for measurable improvement |

**Finding PE-backed targets:**
- Search "[PE firm name] healthcare portfolio" or "[PE firm name] dental"
- Industry publications: Becker's ASC Review, Becker's Dental Review, Group Dentistry Now
- LinkedIn: "VP Operations" + "dental" or "CFO" + "DSO"
